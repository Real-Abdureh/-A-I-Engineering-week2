Tokens are the basic units a transformer works with — chunks of text (roughly words or word-pieces) converted into vectors of numbers. Each token becomes a point in high-dimensional space, capturing some initial sense of its meaning.
Attention is the heart of the transformer. For every token, the model asks: which other tokens in this sequence are relevant to understanding me right now? It computes a score between each pair of tokens (how much should token A "look at" token B?) and uses those scores to pull in context. This is how "bank" in "river bank" learns to look away from "money" and toward "river."
Transformer blocks stack attention on top of a feed-forward network, with normalization in between. Each block refines the token representations — early layers might pick up syntax, later layers capture semantics and long-range relationships. After N such layers, the final representations are rich enough to predict the next token, classify a sentence, or generate a translation.
